Safety Rules

Number 1

A responsible adult must supervise the inflatable at all times. Do not allow children to enter the inflatable without 100% constant adult supervision.

Number 2

All riders must remove shoes, jewelry, eyeglasses, combs, hair clips, or any other sharp or hard objects that may cause injury to children or the inflatable prior to entering any inflatable. (dry rentals, best if kids wear socks for sanitary reasons)

Number 3

No silly string or confetti

Number 4

No hanging on the water hose, safety nets, or basketball rims. Damage repair costs will range from $80 to $225.

Number 5

Max 6-8 kids at a time on any inflatable

Number 6

Max 3 kids or one adult at the top of slides at a time absolutely no food, drink, gum, candy, or silly string inside inflatable

Number 7

No flips, wrestling, running, pushing, climbing nets or walls, or any other aggressive behavior that could injure another child. Do not let riders bounce against the side netting, sides, or entrance of inflatable. No jumping off the top of slides

Number 8

The slide landing area should be clear before the next child goes down the slide.

Number 9

Only children of comparable age and size should use the inflatable at the same time. Mixing children of different sizes will greatly increase the risk of injury.

Number 10

It is not recommended for children to sit or lay down while other children are bouncing around them, as a jumping child could fall on top of a sitting or lying child causing injury.

Number 11

Water added to dry units will result in a cost from $50 to $100, according to the size of the unit.

Number 12

If the blower motor stops: have the children calmly exit the inflatable. Most often, the cause is an overloaded circuit or a piece of debris in front of the blower intake. Reset the circuit breaker and make sure the blower is plugged into a dedicated circuit. Clear any debris away from the intake on the blower.

Number 13

Failure to use good judgment and to follow these safety rules can result in serious injury. The operator must always ensure the inflatable is properly staked and/or weighted down & supervised by an adult insuring all safety rules are followed accordingly.
